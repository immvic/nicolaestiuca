Overview

International Instrumental Competition “Nicolae Știuca”, 6th Edition

Organizers

The competition and master classes are organized by the Cahul Municipal Philharmonic and the Cahul City Hall.

In collaboration with the “Maria Cebotari” Music School in Cahul, the National Commission of the Republic of Moldova for UNESCO, the “Crescendo International” Foundation, the Union of Musicians of the Republic of Moldova, Teleradio Moldova, and the Ministry of Culture of the Republic of Moldova.

Master Classes

Date: Saturday, 2 May, 09:00–10:30 (2 hours).

Instruments: piano, violin, harp, accordion, dulcimer, flute, bagpipes, and musical disciplines.

Teachers from Moldovan music schools seeking advanced training are invited. Participants receive a diploma indicating the topic and hours.

Groups & Categories

Group A: Music School Students.

Group B: High School and College Students.

Age categories: I (≤10), II (11–14), III (15–18). Category IV: performance with symphony orchestra (≤25).

Participation Conditions

General rules

  1. The competition includes four instrument groups: piano, string instruments, wind instruments, and folk instruments. It is intended for children and adolescents up to 18 years of age. The participation fee for categories I–III is 300 MDL.
  2. For the symphony orchestra performance category, the maximum age is 25. The participation fee for category IV is 1000 MDL.
  3. Participants must email the completed registration form, a copy of their birth certificate or ID card, and proof of bank payment.
  4. The schedule and order of entry will be announced in advance and posted at the “Maria Cebotari” Music School and the “Nicolae Botgros” Palace of Culture.
  5. Competitors must wear stage attire during the competition.
  6. The competition is open to the public.
  7. Rehearsals in the competition hall are not provided.
  8. An accompanist for string and wind instrument candidates is not provided.
  9. Transportation, accommodation, and meals are the responsibility of participants.
